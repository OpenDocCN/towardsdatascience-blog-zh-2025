Maybe you’re reading this edition of the Variable from a sandy beach. Maybe you’re just taking a quick break from sending out a batch of data science job applications. Either way, we firmly believe that you deserve some engaging, enlightening reads this week.

At its best, summer reading invites us to take a step back from our daily chores and to explore new topics and questions. It should also, ideally, be fun. The articles we’ve selected for you deliver on all counts, whether you’re interested in the history of data or curious about the next frontier of generative AI. 

Before we dive in, we also wanted to give you a heads-up: we’ve just relaunched our Author Spotlight series, where members of our community talk about their career paths in data science and AI. Don’t miss our first Q&A, with Mauro Di Pietro, where we chat about the power of open source and building from scratch (among other topics).

What Can the History of Data Tell Us About the Future of AI?

Steve Hedden‘s panoramic look at the past 40 years of data, business models, and intelligent systems is essential reading for anyone who’d like to make sense of our present moment, and gain insight into where we might be headed. (Yes, it’s a long read — but one you might enjoy even more over the course of a few days.)

Simple Guide to Multi-Armed Bandits: A Key Concept Before Reinforcement Learning

How does AI learn to make better decisions? Sarah Schürch offers an accessible introduction to a quintessential model.

Work Data Is the Next Frontier for GenAI

“Work data, the work output of knowledge workers, is the single most valuable data source for LLM training.” Zsombor Varnagy-Toth shares a compelling call to action for data, AI, and product leaders.

Welcome Back, Returning Authors!

While we’re as committed as ever to finding new and exciting voices, it’s always a joy to share work by longstanding authors we haven’t published in a while. Case in point:
